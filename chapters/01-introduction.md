# Chapter 1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

## What Is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science focused on building systems that can perform tasks that would typically require human intelligence. These tasks include reasoning, learning, problem-solving, understanding language, perceiving the environment, and making decisions.

AI is not a single technology; it is a broad collection of approaches, algorithms, and applications that share the common goal of enabling machines to act intelligently.

## A Brief History of AI

| Era | Milestone |
|-----|-----------|
| 1950 | Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" as a measure of machine intelligence |
| 1956 | The Dartmouth Conference coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" |
| 1960s–70s | Early symbolic AI and expert systems |
| 1980s | Revival through expert systems; first AI winter ends |
| 1990s | Machine learning gains traction; Deep Blue defeats Kasparov (1997) |
| 2000s | Big data and improved hardware revive neural network research |
| 2012 | Deep learning breakthrough: AlexNet wins ImageNet competition |
| 2017 | Transformer architecture published ("Attention Is All You Need") |
| 2020s | Large language models (GPT, BERT, LLaMA) transform the field |

## Types of AI

### By Capability

- **Narrow AI (Weak AI):** Designed to perform a specific task (e.g., image recognition, playing chess). All current AI systems fall into this category.
- **General AI (Strong AI):** Hypothetical systems that can perform any intellectual task a human can. Does not yet exist.
- **Superintelligent AI:** Hypothetical systems that exceed human intelligence across all domains. A subject of active debate and research.

### By Approach

- **Symbolic AI:** Uses explicit rules and logic (e.g., expert systems, knowledge graphs).
- **Machine Learning:** Learns patterns from data without being explicitly programmed.
- **Hybrid AI:** Combines symbolic reasoning with machine learning.

## Why AI Matters

AI is transforming every major industry:

- **Healthcare:** Disease diagnosis, drug discovery, personalized medicine
- **Finance:** Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, credit scoring
- **Transportation:** Autonomous vehicles, route optimization, traffic management
- **Education:** Personalized learning, automated grading, tutoring systems
- **Science:** Protein structure prediction (AlphaFold), climate modeling, materials discovery

## Key Concepts to Know

- **Model:** A mathematical representation learned from data.
- **Training:** The process of learning patterns from a dataset.
- **Inference:** Using a trained model to make predictions on new data.
- **Feature:** An individual measurable property used as input to a model.
- **Label:** The output or answer a model is trying to predict.
- **Dataset:** A collection of examples used for training and evaluation.
